# Workflow Surfaces

Stonewall is persuasive because it changes the next move, not just because it remembers the last one. Its workflow surfaces are where the archive turns into leverage.

## Notion as operator layer

The repository keeps the durable archive. Notion becomes the live command surface where deadlines, archive links, matter posture, and task runway become legible at the pace of real work.

## DataGavel packet readiness

A strong legal intelligence system should make downstream report workflows easier, not harder. Stonewall stages chronology, treatment trail, and damages notes early so packet assembly becomes an acceleration step rather than a reconstruction project.

## Live deposition tailoring

Because transcripts, emails, filings, and notes all live in one indexed corpus, witness preparation can tighten in real time. Prior statements, chronology gaps, and issue clusters can reshape the next section of an outline while the witness is still in the room.

## Daily dossier

The platform’s daily value comes from immediate situational awareness:

- what changed
- what matters now
- what is nearly ready
- what is missing
- what should happen next

## Deadline intelligence

Stonewall turns dates into runway. Upcoming pressure points can be viewed in relation to the documents, issues, and workflow stages that make those dates matter.

## Why these surfaces matter

This is where commercial viability lives. Buyers care when expensive recurring workflows become less chaotic, less duplicative, and easier to steer. Stonewall’s workflow surfaces attack exactly that pain.
